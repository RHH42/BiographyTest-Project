Spurgeon was the youngest of 6 surviving children of Franklin Pierce Hutchins and Mary Tabor Hutchins. A younger brother, William, died in infancy. His older siblings were Robert Leroy "Lee", Amanda Jane, Lydia Elizabeth, James Alfred "Jim", and John Henry. His father died when he was 4 years old. His mother placed him and his brothers,James and John, in the Baptist orphanage (later called the Baptist Children's Home) at Thomasville, North Carolina where they received primary and secondary schooling. Spurgeon matriculated at Mars Hill College in 1910 where he remained for 2 terms before leaving just before his 17th birthday.

Spurgeon became a printer, starting when movable type was still in use and later becoming a Mergenthaler Lin-O-Type machine operator. He worked at different times for Edwards & Broughton Printing Co., The News & Observer, and Bynum Printing Co. where he set type for NC Supreme Court and Superior Court proceedings. He was a member of the International Typographical Union for more than 40 years before retiring in 1959.
